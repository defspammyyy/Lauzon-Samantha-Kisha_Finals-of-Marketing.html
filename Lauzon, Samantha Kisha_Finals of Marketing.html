About Valderrama

Valderrama is a beautiful mountain town in Antique with a history that goes back to 1865. Long before it became a municipality, it was a small village originally called Caberi-an. The town was eventually named after Manuel Blanco Valderrama, a Spanish official who once served as the Governor-General of the Philippines. While its name comes from history books, its heart belongs to the Iraynon-Bukidnon people. This area is their ancestral home, where they have lived in harmony with the mountains for generations. Today, the town is dedicated to a special mission: growing a type of tourism that is kind to nature and respectful of the local culture, making sure the traditions and forests of Valderrama stay protected for the future.

The Conscious Explorer

This destination is perfectly designed for travelers aged 18 to 35 who are looking for more than just a typical vacation. Valderrama appeals to young adventurists and cultural enthusiasts who prefer hidden gems over crowded tourist spots. It offers a unique mix of high-energy outdoor activities and deep cultural experiences, making it a great fit for those who care about the planet. By choosing this "off-the-beaten-path" journey, travelers get to immerse themselves in the local way of life while supporting environmental conservation. It is a place for a new generation of explorers who want their travels to be authentic, sustainable, and meaningful.

Strengths: Authentic culture and heritage, Untouched landscapes, and Sustainable tourism practices.
Weaknesses: Limited accessibility (remote location), Low digital footprint, and Seasonal accessibility.
Opportunities: Growth in eco-tourism, Slow travel trends, and Partnerships with local communities.
Threats: Environmental damage, Climate change effects on trails, and Over-tourism at sacred sites.

Must-Visit Attractions

The Valderrama Trilogy Hike

The Valderrama Trilogy is an intense trekking challenge that covers three major peaks: Mt. Igcoron, Mt. Igduao, and Mt. Taripis. This circuit offers a diverse landscape ranging from sharp limestone ridges and rolling grasslands to dense tropical forests. Depending on the hiker's pace and weather conditions, the full trilogy typically takes 10 to 14 hours to complete, often starting well before dawn. It is highly recommended for experienced hikers seeking a test of endurance and breathtaking 360-degree views of the Antique mountain range.

Mt.Baloy

Known as one of the most difficult climbs in the Panay mountain range, Mt. Baloy is a sacred and majestic peak that borders Antique and Iloilo. It offers a raw, mossy forest environment and is home to unique flora and fauna, including the world‚Äôs largest flower, the Rafflesia. This is a multi-day expedition rather than a simple trek, usually requiring 3 to 4 days of hiking to reach the summit and return. It is a bucket-list destination for mountaineers looking for deep wilderness immersion.

Antique Rice Terraces

The Antique Rice Terraces (located in the highlands of the Valderrama-San Remigio border) are a cultural treasure hand-carved by the Iraynon-Bukidnon people. These emerald-green steps showcase the indigenous engineering and agricultural heritage of the region. To reach the best viewing decks from the Valderrama side, visitors should prepare for a trek of approximately 4 to 5 hours. The site offers a peaceful atmosphere and a rare look at a living cultural landscape that remains largely untouched by modernization.

Villa Valderrama Mountain Resort

A local mountain resort that serves as the primary base camp and jump-off point for tourists and hikers. It offers refreshing pools fed by natural springs and native-style huts for an authentic overnight stay.

Kawa-Kawa Falls

Located near the resort, these falls feature unique natural rock formations that look like giant cooking pots, or kawa. The water is crystal clear and cool, making it a perfect spot for a quick dip after a long day of exploring. It is an easy, short walk from the main resort area, so no heavy trekking is required to enjoy it.

How to Get Here?

To reach Valderrama, Antique, follow these travel steps:

1. Air Travel

Fly to nearby airports like Iloilo (ILO) or Caticlan (MPH) for Boracay.

2. Land Transport

Take a bus or van from the airport terminals to Valderrama town center.

3. Local Access

Hire a local tricycle or habal-habal (motorcycle taxi) to reach these must-visit destinations.

Note: Accommodations are basic, consisting of local guesthouses and the Villa Valderrama Mountain Resort.
